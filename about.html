Welcome to TUNESF

The Tunisian Federation of eSports (TUNESF) is the official governing body for eSports in Tunisia, established in 2019 with the mission of organizing, regulating, and promoting the digital sports ecosystem across the country. Recognized by national authorities as an official sports federation, TUNeSF plays a key role in structuring competitive gaming and supporting players, clubs, and stakeholders.

The Federation oversees 13 official tournaments per sports season, bringing together 26 affiliated sports associations. These events include national competitions and qualifiers for prestigious regional and global championships. Tunisia has already marked major milestones, such as winning the African Championship in Counter Strike 2 and qualifying for the 2024 World Championship in Riyadh. Additionally, the national women’s teams proudly earned silver medals in Mobile Legends BB and Counter Strike 2, affirming Tunisia's place on the international eSports stage.

Community Engagement and Events

Beyond competitions, the Federation is active in community and citizenship initiatives. It organizes outreach programs, including visits to SOS Children’s Villages and awareness days in institutions hosting vulnerable groups.

The Federation also hosts major events such as the Tunisia Gaming Show and Bigtech Exhibition, offering platforms for companies and players in the gaming industry. Additionally, it invests in capacity building through internships and international training camps—such as the referee and competition manager training held in May 2024 under the supervision of the International Federation of eSports.

ur Vision

Finally, eSports is a practice that is witnessing increasing numbers and widespread interest. It has contributed to the development of the economies of many countries, moving beyond the concept of consumption to the idea of ​​contributing to the development of the economic system through the development of equipment or games, becoming one of the fundamental pillars for guiding economic policies and enabling youth to showcase their skills and creativity.

From this perspective, the Tunisian Federation of eSports passed a law establishing the National Committee for the Game Industry, whose main tasks include supporting private initiatives and emerging companies in the field of game development and manufacturing, developing competencies in this field, and organizing local and international forums.
